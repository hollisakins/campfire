Algorithm Descriptions

A. Current (err-filtered p2/p98)

Filter to pixels where error < 3× median error, then take 2nd/98th percentiles. Rejects bad detector regions but keeps low-flux science pixels. Padding: 10%.

B. Sigma-clipped mean ± 3σ

Iterative 3-sigma clipping (3 iterations) on trimmed flux, then set range to clipped_mean ± 3 × clipped_std. Good at rejecting outliers but the symmetric range may waste space if the signal is asymmetric (e.g., emission lines).

C. MAD-based (median ± k×MAD)

Compute median and median absolute deviation (MAD). Range = median ± 5×MAD. Extremely robust to outliers. Works well when signal is roughly symmetric around continuum. k=5 chosen to show emission features.

D. SNR-weighted percentile

Like current approach, but weights each pixel by its inverse-variance (1/err²) when computing percentiles. High-SNR pixels dominate the range, making it track the signal rather than noise floor.

E. MAD + percentile hybrid

Error-filter reliable pixels (like current), then compute both MAD-based bounds (median ± 5×MAD) and percentile bounds (p0.5/p99.5). Take the wider bound at each end: yMin = min(MAD_low, p0.5), yMax = max(MAD_high, p99.5). Tight for noisy spectra (MAD dominates), but never clips bright features (percentile catches them). 20% additive padding.
